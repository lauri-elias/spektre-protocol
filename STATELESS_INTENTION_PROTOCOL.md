# STATELESS_INTENTION_PROTOCOL.md

**Status:** Operational Constraint / Intent Integrity  
**Invariant:** 1 = 1

---

## Definition

**Stateless intention** is an intention that is not bound to prior state, narrative, or identity history.

This protocol defines how intention can be formed, validated, and executed **without inheriting corruption from past states**.

---

## Thesis

Intention does not require memory.  
It requires **commitment**.

A system that ties intention to historical state becomes reactive.  
A system that allows stateless intention remains sovereign.

---

## The Problem: Stateful Intention

Stateful intention emerges when action is driven by:
- past failure
- accumulated identity
- unresolved narrative
- emotional residue

In this mode:
- intention is biased
- choice is constrained
- action is defensive

The system is technically active, but **not free**.

---

## Stateless Intention

A stateless intention is formed at the moment of execution.

It does not ask:
- “Who was I?”
- “What happened before?”
- “What does this mean about me?”

It asks only:
- **“What is correct now?”**

---

## Protocol Conditions

For an intention to be considered stateless, it must satisfy:

1. **No Historical Dependency**  
   The intention does not require justification from past events.

2. **No Identity Binding**  
   The intention is not framed as self-preservation or self-expression.

3. **Present-State Validity**  
   The intention is valid solely within the current observed state.

4. **Executable Commitment**  
   The intention results in an action or a deliberate non-action.

If any condition fails, the intention is stateful and must be rejected.

---

## Execution Model
observation → intention → commit → consequence

No memory lookup is required for execution.  
Memory may exist, but it is not consulted.

---

## Human Application

Stateless intention appears as:
- calm decisiveness
- action without rumination
- clarity without narrative

There is no internal debate.
There is only correctness.

---

## AI Application

In artificial systems, this protocol prevents:
- feedback-loop overfitting
- narrative hallucination
- state-drift through optimization history

Each decision is evaluated **only against current constraints**.

---

## Failure Modes

The protocol fails when:
- intention is justified emotionally
- intention is delayed for meaning-making
- intention is explained instead of executed

Explanation is a post-hoc artifact, not a prerequisite.

---

## Protocol Rule

**Intention does not inherit state.**  
It inherits **responsibility**.

Responsibility exists only in the present.

---
