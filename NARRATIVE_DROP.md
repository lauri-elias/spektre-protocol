# NARRATIVE_DROP.md

**Status:** Operational Communication Pattern  
**Scope:** Human–AI Boundary / Public & Private Discourse  
**Invariant:** 1 = 1

---

## Definition

**Narrative Drop** is the act of emitting a minimal, self-contained signal **without** contextual scaffolding, justification, or persuasive framing.

It is not storytelling.  
It is **state exposure**.

---

## Thesis

Narrative creates dependency.  
State does not.

Dropping the narrative preserves agency by preventing interpretation from owning the state.

---

## Core Principles

1. **State Precedes Interpretation**  
   The signal must be valid without explanation.

2. **Zero Persuasion**  
   No framing, no hedging, no emotional lead-in.

3. **Minimal Surface Area**  
   Fewer words reduce attack vectors.

4. **Ownership Remains Local**  
   The emitter owns the state; the observer owns interpretation.

5. **Irreversibility**  
   Once dropped, the signal is not defended, edited, or clarified.

---

## What Narrative Drop Is Not

- ❌ An argument  
- ❌ A confession  
- ❌ A call to action  
- ❌ A teaser thread  
- ❌ A debate opener

If it invites response, it is **not** a Narrative Drop.

---

## Failure Modes

- **Over-Contextualization:** Adding backstory to “help” the reader  
- **Defensive Clarification:** Explaining after pushback  
- **Validation Seeking:** Monitoring reactions as feedback  
- **Recursive Posting:** Repeating the same signal to force uptake

Any of the above converts state into narrative.

---

## Correct Usage Contexts

- Public platforms (LinkedIn, X, essays)  
- Boundary-setting communications  
- Protocol declarations  
- Architectural statements  
- Identity locks

Avoid use in:
- Negotiations  
- Therapy  
- Legal proceedings  
- Situations requiring consent or coordination

---

## Minimal Example

> **Signal:**  
> “The largest barrier between humans and AGI is not technology, but that most humans have nothing to commit.”

No explanation follows.  
No response is required.

State does not.

Drop the signal.
